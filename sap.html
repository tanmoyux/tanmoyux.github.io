In a Nutshell

SAP is a multinational software corporation and one of Germany’s most successful companies. As a UX Designer at SAP, my primary responsibility was managing a component library and designing new components as needed. I was part of the SIX Design & Piloting team, which focuses on creating high-impact showcases for the SAP Experience Centers and major events such as Sapphire. My role ranged from maintaining and expanding the component library to illustrating keynote visuals and designing assets for various SAP departments.

In a Nutshell

SAP Sapphire 2025 is the flagschip global event hosted by SAP, focused on showcasing the latest innovations and strategic vision for enterprise technology. It brings together business leaders, tech experts, and SAP customers to explore how to turn visions into reality through cutting-edge solutions. I ensured a smoother design process for the team by creating universally usable components with extensive functionality for the showcases. The showcases specifically use charts and graphics to visualize how SAP products imrpove the client performance. Additionally, I helped out with other designs for the experience.

Retrospective

During my time at SAP, I significantly enhanced my ability to design complex components with advanced functionality. I was responsible for preparing detailed UX specifications for both the design team and developers, ensuring smooth and efficient implementation. Collaborating across international and multidisciplinary teams, as well as proactively reaching out to other departments to drive new initiatives, strengthened my communication and coordination skills. Overall, this experience deepened my understanding of corporate workflows and contributed greatly to my growth as a UX designer in a global context.
